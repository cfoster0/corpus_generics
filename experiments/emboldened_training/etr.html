Instructions

Imagine saying the following sentences to yourself, emphasizing the part in blue.

Sentence 1: Bob went to the movies.

In this sentence, the emphasis is on Bob. This emphasis draws attention to the noun and suggests that Bob (as opposed to other people) went to the movies.

Sentence 2: Bob went to the movies.

In this sentence, the emphasis is on what Bob did (namely, "went to the movies"). This emphasis draws attention to the action and suggests that Bob went to the movies as opposed to doing other things.

In this experiment, you will read a bit of a conversation between two individuals and be asked to identify what emphasis a speaker intends to make.
